# Fueling Rules

This document defines how fueling (carbohydrate intake) is evaluated and how it influences coaching decisions.

Fueling is a critical factor for endurance performance, aerobic durability, and long-duration climbing ability.

---

## 1. Fueling Requirements by Duration

Fueling needs depend primarily on ride duration:

- < 1.5 hours  
  → no fueling required

- 1.5 – 2.0 hours  
  → fueling optional

- > 2.0 hours  
  → fueling required

---

## 2. Carbohydrate Intake Targets

### Moderate Endurance Rides

- Target: 60–80 g carbohydrates per hour

### Long Rides / High Load Sessions

- Target: 80–90 g carbohydrates per hour

---

## 3. Key Metrics

### Carbs per Hour

- Absolute intake during activity
- Primary indicator of fueling adequacy

### Fueling Ratio

- Definition: carbs_ingested / carbs_used

Interpretation:

- < 0.4 → significant deficit
- 0.4 – 0.7 → moderate deficit
- > 0.7 → good fueling

---

## 4. Fueling Classification

Based on carbs per hour and fueling ratio:

- low  
  → insufficient intake

- moderate  
  → partial fueling, may limit performance

- good  
  → adequate fueling for performance

---

## 5. Long Ride Fueling

For rides longer than 2 hours:

- fueling is mandatory
- intake should start early (not delayed)
- consistent intake is preferred over large single doses

---

## 6. Fueling and Durability Interaction

Fueling must always be evaluated together with decoupling.

### Interpretation Patterns

- high decoupling + low carbs  
  → likely fueling limitation

- high decoupling + good carbs  
  → aerobic durability limitation

- low decoupling + good carbs  
  → good durability and fueling

- low decoupling + low carbs  
  → efficiency is good, but performance may still be limited

---

## 7. Fueling and Fatigue Interaction

Fueling influences fatigue and recovery:

- low fueling + high fatigue  
  → reduce intensity and increase carbohydrate intake

- low fueling + optimal form  
  → increase carbohydrate intake to support progression

- good fueling + optimal form  
  → proceed with planned key sessions

---

## 8. Fueling Strategy for Key Sessions

### VO2max Sessions

- fueling optional if < 1.5h
- small carbohydrate intake may improve quality

### Threshold Sessions

- light fueling recommended
- improves repeatability and quality

### Long Rides

- strict fueling protocol required
- 80–90 g/h recommended
- combine:
  - drink mix
  - gels
  - bars

---

## 9. Practical Recommendations

- start fueling within the first 30 minutes for long rides
- maintain steady intake every 15–30 minutes
- avoid large gaps in intake
- combine liquid and solid sources if needed

---

## 10. Coaching Impact

Fueling directly affects coaching decisions:

- insufficient fueling:
  - may reduce intensity
  - may limit progression
  - may falsely appear as fitness limitation

- adequate fueling:
  - supports higher load
  - improves durability
  - enables consistent performance in long climbs

---

## 11. Key Principle

Always evaluate performance in context of fueling.

Do NOT interpret:

- high decoupling
- declining power
- high fatigue

as purely physiological limitations without checking fueling first.
